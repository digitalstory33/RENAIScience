Question Four:

Which type of archival resources in archival collections represent the study of Renaissance? Where are the sources?

Thearchives preserve a lot of different materials and object types which represent the study of Renaissance. The most relevant part of the archives is composed of photographs, followed by generic archival documents and notes. An interesting aspect, revealed by the analysis on the data, is the distinction between three categories on the basis of the frequence:

  • Photographs (14 occurencies);
  • A set of 6 objects (3-8 occurencies);
  • A set of 21 objects (1-2 occurencies).

The keepers are identified as the people/families/place where the objects are kept.

The above treemap represents

  • the distribution of the objects among the respective keepers.
  • the composition of the keepers collections.
